EduFrame Privacy Policy

Last updated: 20 July 2026 · App: EduFrame (com.eduframe.app)

EduFrame is a lesson-plan notebook for teachers. This policy explains what data we collect, how it is used, and your choices.

1. Data we store on your device

Lesson plans, classes, and timetable entries are stored locally in an SQLite database on your device. Appearance settings (dark mode, Hindi labels) and notification preferences are stored in on-device preferences. This data stays on your device unless you choose to back it up or export it.

2. Google account

Sign-in uses Google Sign-In. We receive your Google account identifier, name, email, and profile photo so we can authenticate you and show your account in Settings. We do not operate a separate EduFrame cloud account database for lesson plans.

3. AI Assist (Groq)

When you use AI Assist or Improve, the prompts and lesson context you send are transmitted to our proxy at eduframe.vercel.app and then to Groq’s API to generate a response. Do not include sensitive student personal data in AI prompts. AI requests require a valid Google sign-in token.

4. Backups

If you use Backup to Google Drive or share a backup file, your exported lesson data leaves the device under your control (Drive or the share target you choose). Restore replaces local data with the backup contents.

5. Notifications

With reminders enabled, EduFrame schedules local notifications (for example, shortly before a class period). Notification permission is requested by the operating system. Reminder content is generated on-device from your timetable.

6. Crash reporting

If crash reporting is enabled for a build, anonymous crash and diagnostic data may be sent to our crash reporting provider (for example Sentry) to help fix bugs. This does not include your full lesson plan database.

7. Data deletion

In Settings you can delete all local EduFrame data for your signed-in account (classes, plans, timetable) and then sign out. Uninstalling the app also removes on-device data. Google account deletion is managed by Google. Drive backups you created remain in your Drive until you delete them.

8. Children

EduFrame is intended for teachers (adults), not for children under 13.

9. Changes

We may update this policy. The “Last updated” date above will change when we do.

10. Contact

Questions about privacy: open an issue on the EduFrame GitHub repository or contact the developer listed on the Google Play store listing.
